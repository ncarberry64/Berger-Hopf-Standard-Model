## Physicality ontology

`BHSM_PHYSICALITY_ONTOLOGY_FROZEN`

Physicality is the observable differentiation produced when energy modifies,
strains, localizes, propagates through, or envelopes spacetime. This is a BHSM
geometric reinterpretation, not a new dynamical theorem.

The minimal dictionary is:

- physical differential: a nonzero change in stress, energy, flux, curvature,
  field value, topology, or boundary response relative to a declared
  background;
- envelopment: a localized, bounded, shell-like, level-set, or otherwise
  distinguishable spacetime differential;
- persistence: continued existence under the equations of motion;
- objecthood: self-supported persistent localization;
- particle-like object: objecthood together with the required quantization,
  topology, spectrum, spin/statistics, charges, and interactions.

Neither nonzero sigma nor harmonic interference is required for physicality in
general. The scientific burden on BHSM is the Berger--Hopf-specific derivation
of discrete structures, coefficients, spectra, forces, particles,
generations, scales, and predictions.
